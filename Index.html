MY STORY

I got started as a photographer way back in 2017, when I was 20 years old.

My first serious camera was a Canon 200D, lent to me by a local event photographer (thanks, Favas!!!). After months of research, I went all-in on a 70-300mm lens. I wanted to photograph birds, but I soon realized that even 300mm wasn’t long enough for frame-filling bird images (not where I lived in the Pondichery, anyway!).

That’s when I bought my first macro lens. I figured that if I couldn’t photograph distant subjects, I could get interesting photos of beautiful subjects that were in my home garden, such as flowers and insects. I couldn’t afford a fancy new model, so I purchased an old, used 24mm apexel model. The lens wasn’t in the best shape, but I didn’t care; I was instantly hooked on macro photography, and for years, that was pretty much the only lens I used.

Since then, I’ve expanded into other genres of photography, including landscape, still life, street, architecture, and portrait. I’ve also rediscovered my love for bird photography (though I had to make a few equipment upgrades along the way!).
